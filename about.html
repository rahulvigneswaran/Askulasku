Origin story

The song that said
love in every language.

There's a Tamil song called Asku Lasku (ஆஸ்கு லாஸ்கு). Inside it, something quietly extraordinary happens: the word love is sung in language after language — Spanish, French, Hindi, Arabic, Japanese — one right after the other.

Hearing the same feeling cross so many tongues in a few seconds is genuinely moving. It makes an abstract idea concrete: some things don't need translation to be felt, but hearing them in translation is its own kind of beauty.

That moment was the seed of this site. What if you could take any phrase and send it through every major language in the world — not as a table in a dictionary, but as something you could watch and share?

How it works

Type a word or phrase in English. The site sends it to Google Translate for each of 30+ languages simultaneously, then plays it back as a split-flap display — like an airport departure board — flipping through every language in five seconds.

Tamil is always shown first. It's the one that started all of this.
